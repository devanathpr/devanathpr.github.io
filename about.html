About

I studied Sociology for my Bachelor's, then ended up in Library Science for my Master's — not by choice, but by chance😄. The chance turned out very well: I left with a gold medal, and a career I didn't see coming.

At present, I work as a Scientific Assistant (Library) at the International Centre for Theoretical Sciences (ICTS-TIFR), Bangalore, a world-class research institute in India, where I manage the institute's library. Before this, I spent close to three years each at Dayananda Sagar University and at NITTE's Dr. N.S.A.M. First Grade College, in similar roles.

Landing that first job gave me a kind of confidence I hadn't expected. Somewhere along the way, a growing interest in technology changed the shape of my work entirely. I hadn't used a computer with any real fluency until after my Master's but now I work with one, and through one, every day.

Reading has shaped that journey as much as work has. Books, are what pushed me toward being more open-minded and a little more empathetic than I started out.

Doctoral research

I'm currently enrolled in a part-time Ph.D. programme at Tumkur University, working on a thesis in research productivity and impact of Indian-funded research. Most theses end up archived in a university repository where almost no one looks twice — I'm hoping mine does a little more than that😜.

My scope at present is limited to quantitative evaluation of research performance.
