About

A librarian's route into research

I studied Sociology for my Bachelor's, then ended up in Library Science for my Master's — not by choice, but by chance. The chance turned out well: I left with a gold medal, and a career I didn't see coming.

I work as a Scientific Assistant (Library) at the International Centre for Theoretical Sciences (ICTS-TIFR), Bangalore, where I manage the institute's library. Before this, I spent close to three years each at Dayananda Sagar University and at NITTE's Dr. N.S.A.M. First Grade College, in similar roles.

Landing that first job gave me a kind of confidence I hadn't expected. Somewhere along the way, a growing interest in technology changed the shape of my work entirely. I hadn't used a computer with any real fluency until after my Master's — now I work with one, and through one, every day.

Reading has shaped that journey as much as work has. Books, more than coursework, are what pushed me toward being more open-minded and a little more empathetic than I started out.

Doctoral research

I'm currently enrolled in a part-time Ph.D. programme at Tumkur University, working on a thesis in research evaluation. Most theses end up archived in a university repository where almost no one looks twice — I'm hoping mine does a little more than that.

Within that, two threads occupy most of my attention. The first is understanding what research evaluation actually captures: the standard scientometric and altmetric measures are a starting point, but "gaming the metrics" has become a real strain on scholarly publishing, which is pushing me to think about qualitative dimensions of evaluation too. The second is research ethics — specifically, citation patterns and behaviour around retracted publications, with a focus on research funded within India.
